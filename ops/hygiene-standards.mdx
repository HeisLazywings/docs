Food hygiene is not optional — it is the foundation of everything we do. Every staff member is legally and professionally responsible for maintaining the highest standards of food safety at all times.

## Personal Hygiene

These rules apply to every person working in or entering the kitchen, without exception.

- Wash hands with soap for at least 20 seconds before handling food, after using the toilet, and after handling raw meat.
- Keep fingernails short and clean. No nail varnish in the kitchen.
- Cover all cuts and wounds with a blue waterproof plaster.
- Do not work in the kitchen if you have a contagious illness, skin infection, vomiting, or diarrhoea.
- No eating, drinking (except water), or chewing gum in food preparation areas.

<Note>
  Report any illness to your supervisor immediately. You will not be penalised — your customers' safety comes first.
</Note>

## Food Handling Rules

Safe food handling prevents contamination and protects both customers and staff.

- Always use separate chopping boards for raw meat and ready-to-eat foods.
- Never place cooked or ready-to-eat food on surfaces that have held raw meat.
- Use tongs, gloves, or scoops for handling food — not bare hands where possible.
- Check use-by dates on all ingredients before use. Never use expired stock.
- Thaw frozen items in the fridge overnight — never at room temperature.

<Warning>
  Cross-contamination between raw and cooked food is one of the leading causes of food poisoning. Take it seriously.
</Warning>

## Food Storage Temperatures

Storing food at the correct temperature prevents bacterial growth and spoilage. Refer to this table whenever you are unsure.

| Storage Zone | Temperature | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Freezer | -18°C or below | Frozen dough, frozen meat |
| Chiller / Fridge | 0°C – 5°C | Dairy, fresh meat, drinks |
| Dry Store | Cool, dry, 10–15°C | Flour, sugar, dry spices |
| Hot Holding | 63°C or above | Freshly baked items for service |

## Kitchen Cleaning Schedule

Stick to this schedule every day. A clean kitchen is a safe kitchen.

| Task | Frequency | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| Wipe down prep surfaces | After each use | All kitchen staff |
| Wash utensils and equipment | After each use | All kitchen staff |
| Mop kitchen floor | End of every shift | Closing staff |
| Clean oven interior | Daily (after last use) | Bakery team |
| Deep clean all equipment | Weekly (Fridays) | All staff |
| Check fridge temperatures | Twice daily (open & close) | Supervisor |

## Pest Control

Pests are attracted to food environments. Your vigilance is the first line of defence.

- Keep all dry goods in sealed containers.
- Report any signs of pests (droppings, gnaw marks, live insects) to the supervisor immediately.
- Never leave food uncovered overnight.
- Do not prop open back doors unnecessarily.
