Every customer interaction reflects on 5G Silver Square as a whole. Your warmth, speed, and professionalism at the counter are just as important as the quality of the food we serve.

## Greeting Customers

First impressions matter. Make every customer feel genuinely welcome from the moment they walk in.

- Greet every customer with a smile and a warm "Welcome to 5G Silver Bakery!"
- Make eye contact and give the customer your full attention.
- If you are busy, acknowledge the customer and let them know you will be with them shortly.
- Never eat, use your phone, or have side conversations while serving a customer.

## Taking Orders

Accuracy and clarity make the difference between a smooth transaction and a frustrated customer.

- Repeat back the customer's order to confirm accuracy.
- If an item is sold out, apologise and suggest an alternative.
- Clearly communicate the price before the customer pays.
- Always give a receipt if requested.

<Note>
  If a customer asks about allergens (e.g., nuts, gluten, dairy), consult the recipe notes or call the supervisor. Never guess.
</Note>

## WhatsApp & Phone Orders

Remote orders deserve the same care and speed as walk-in service.

- WhatsApp and phone orders should be acknowledged within 5 minutes.
- Confirm the order, total price, and estimated preparation time.
- For delivery orders, confirm the delivery address and estimated arrival time.
- WhatsApp order line: **+234 905 500 0737**

<Tip>
  Keep WhatsApp messages professional and friendly. You represent the brand in every message.
</Tip>

## Handling Complaints

A complaint handled well can turn a dissatisfied customer into a loyal one. Follow these steps every time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Listen">
    Let the customer finish speaking without interrupting. Do not become defensive or dismissive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apologise">
    Offer a sincere apology for the experience, regardless of fault. This costs nothing and defuses most situations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Act">
    Offer a replacement, refund, or solution within your authority. If you cannot resolve it, involve the supervisor immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record">
    Log the complaint in the complaints book: date, customer description, issue, and resolution.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow Up">
    If the customer leaves contact details, the supervisor may follow up to ensure their satisfaction.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Never argue with a customer on the shop floor. If the situation escalates, calmly excuse yourself and get the supervisor.
</Warning>

## Packaging & Presentation

How the food leaves the counter is the last impression a customer takes with them — make it a good one.

- All takeaway items must be placed in a clean, sealed bag or box.
- Label items that look similar to avoid customer confusion.
- Hot items should be wrapped to stay warm during delivery or transit.
- Always check the order is complete before handing it to the customer.
