Good stock control keeps costs down and ensures we never run out of ingredients during a busy service. Every staff member should understand the basics of how we manage inventory.

## Daily Stock Check

Run through these checks first thing every morning before service begins.

- [ ] Check refrigerated items first thing every morning (dairy, fillings, drinks).
- [ ] Check dry goods levels (flour, sugar, yeast, spices, oils).
- [ ] Record current quantities in the daily stock log sheet.
- [ ] Flag any item below the minimum threshold to the supervisor before 8:00 AM.
- [ ] Check freshness of any pre-prepared items from the previous day.

## Minimum Stock Thresholds

When any item reaches its minimum level, you must act immediately. Do not wait until you run out.

| Item | Minimum Stock Level | Action When Reached |
|---|---|---|
| Strong white flour | 5 kg | Re-order immediately |
| Instant dry yeast | 10 sachets | Re-order immediately |
| Sugar | 2 kg | Re-order immediately |
| Vegetable oil | 2 litres | Re-order this week |
| Eggs | 12 (1 tray) | Re-order immediately |
| Margarine / Butter | 500 g | Re-order immediately |
| Minced beef | 500 g | Re-order or use from freezer |

## The FIFO Rule

FIFO stands for **First In, First Out**. It is the golden rule of stock rotation and must be followed every time you restock shelves or storage.

- Always place newer stock behind older stock on shelves.
- Always use the oldest stock first.
- Check dates when restocking and remove anything that has expired.

<Tip>
  Label every delivery with the date received. This makes FIFO easy to apply and eliminates guesswork.
</Tip>

## Ordering Procedures

All ordering must follow a consistent process to avoid duplication, overspending, or missed items.

- All orders must be approved by the supervisor before being placed.
- Keep a running order list on the whiteboard in the store room.
- Orders are typically placed every Monday and Thursday.
- Record all deliveries in the delivery log: item, quantity, date, supplier.

## Waste Management

Waste directly affects the bakery's profitability. Track it carefully and work to reduce it.

- Record all wasted items in the daily waste log (item, quantity, reason).
- Common waste reasons: over-baked, unsold after 24 hours, dropped, damaged.
- Reducing waste is a team responsibility — only bake quantities that will sell.

<Warning>
  Never sell or serve food that has passed its recommended storage time. When in doubt, discard it.
</Warning>
