Starting a new role at 5G Silver Bakery is an exciting step, and this guide is designed to make your first week as smooth as possible. From understanding the layout of the kitchen to knowing who to speak to when you have a question, everything you need to settle in and hit the ground running is right here.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Meet Your Supervisor">
    On your first day, your priority is to find and check in with your assigned supervisor before you do anything else. They will walk you through the basics, introduce you to the rest of the team, and let you know where you will be working. Make sure you check in with your supervisor at the start of every shift — they need to know you are on site and ready to work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Learn Your Station">
    5G Silver Bakery operates across several stations, including baking, prep, counter service, and drinks. Each station has its own rhythm and responsibilities, and it is important to understand how yours fits into the wider operation. Your supervisor will assign you to a specific station based on your role and the needs of the day. Take time to familiarise yourself with the equipment, layout, and expectations of your assigned area.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the Recipes">
    Consistency is everything in a bakery. Once you know your station, head to the **Recipes** section of this documentation to study the standard recipes used at 5G Silver Bakery. Follow them precisely — do not improvise until your supervisor has authorised any changes. If anything is unclear, ask before you proceed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Study Opening & Closing Procedures">
    Every shift has a defined start and end routine that keeps the bakery safe, clean, and ready for customers. Read through the full procedures at [Opening & Closing Procedures](/ops/opening-closing) so you know exactly what is expected of you at the beginning and end of each working day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the Hygiene Standards">
    Food hygiene is non-negotiable at 5G Silver Bakery. Before you handle any food or equipment, make sure you have read and understood the requirements set out in the [Hygiene Standards](/ops/hygiene-standards). These rules protect both you and every customer who walks through our doors.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Always wear your uniform and hair net before entering the kitchen. You should never be in the food preparation area without them, even briefly.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Don't be afraid to ask questions — your supervisor and colleagues are here to help. No question is too small when food quality and safety are at stake.
</Tip>

## Your Daily Schedule

The table below shows a typical bakery day shift at 5G Silver Bakery. Your exact schedule may vary depending on your station and the day's workload, but this gives you a clear picture of how the day flows.

| Time | Activity |
|------|-----------|
| 7:30 AM | Arrive, sign in, and prepare your station for the day |
| 8:00 AM | Begin baking the first batch of the morning |
| 12:00 PM | Lunch break — ensure your station is covered before you leave |
| 1:00 PM | Return from break and begin afternoon prep |
| 6:00 PM | Evening clean-down of your station and equipment |
| 9:00 PM | Final checks, close down, and sign out (Mon–Fri) |

<Note>
  On Saturdays and Sundays, the bakery closes at 10:00 PM. If you are on a weekend shift, your closing duties will run until that time. Always confirm your finish time with your supervisor at the start of the shift.
</Note>
