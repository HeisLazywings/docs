The meat pie is one of our best-selling items. Each pie should be golden, flaky on the outside and filled with a well-seasoned meat and potato filling. Consistency in portion size, seasoning, and bake quality is what keeps customers coming back — follow this recipe exactly every production run.

## Recipe Metadata

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Yield | 20 medium pies |
| Prep Time | 45 minutes |
| Bake Time | 25–30 minutes |
| Total Time | ~75 minutes |
| Difficulty | Intermediate |

## Ingredients

### Shortcrust Pastry

- [ ] 500 g plain flour
- [ ] 250 g cold margarine or butter (cubed)
- [ ] 1 tsp salt
- [ ] 1 tsp baking powder
- [ ] 2 eggs
- [ ] Cold water (as needed, about 4 tbsp)

### Meat Filling

- [ ] 400 g minced beef
- [ ] 2 medium potatoes (diced small)
- [ ] 1 medium carrot (diced small)
- [ ] 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
- [ ] 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- [ ] 1 tsp curry powder
- [ ] 1 tsp thyme
- [ ] 1 beef stock cube
- [ ] Salt and pepper to taste
- [ ] 3 tbsp flour (to thicken filling)
- [ ] 200 ml water

## Step-by-Step Method

<Steps>
  <Step title="Make the Filling">
    Fry the onion in vegetable oil over medium heat until soft and translucent. Add the minced beef and cook, stirring regularly, until fully browned. Add the diced potatoes, carrot, crumbled stock cube, curry powder, thyme, salt, and pepper. Pour in the water and stir to combine. Simmer on low heat for 15 minutes until the vegetables are tender. Sprinkle in the flour and stir thoroughly to thicken the mixture. Remove from heat and allow the filling to **cool completely** before use.

    <Warning>
      Never fill your pastry with hot filling — the heat will melt the dough, causing the pies to collapse and turn soggy in the oven.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Make the Pastry">
    In a large bowl, combine the plain flour, salt, and baking powder. Add the cold cubed margarine (or butter) and rub it into the flour using your fingertips until the mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs. Beat the eggs separately, then add them to the flour mixture and stir to incorporate. Add cold water one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently after each addition, until the dough just comes together into a smooth ball. Do not overwork the dough — this will develop gluten and make the pastry tough rather than flaky.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Rest the Dough">
    Wrap the dough tightly in cling film and transfer it to the refrigerator. Allow it to rest for at least 20 minutes. Chilling firms up the fat within the dough, which is what creates those distinct flaky layers during baking.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Roll & Cut">
    Lightly flour your work surface and rolling pin. Remove the dough from the fridge and roll it out evenly to approximately 4 mm thickness — thin enough to bake through properly but thick enough to hold the filling without tearing. Use a round pastry cutter (approximately 12 cm in diameter) to cut circles from the dough. Re-roll any offcuts and continue cutting until all the dough is used.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill & Seal">
    Place one level tablespoon of the cooled filling onto one half of each pastry circle, leaving a clear border around the edge. Fold the empty half of the circle over the filling to form a half-moon shape. Press the edges together firmly, then crimp all the way around using the back of a fork to create a tight seal.

    <Note>
      Ensure every pie's edges are well sealed before baking. Any gaps will allow the filling to leak out during the bake, resulting in a messy tray and dry pies.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Egg Wash & Bake">
    Arrange the sealed pies on a lined baking tray, spacing them slightly apart. Beat one egg and brush the tops of each pie generously with the egg wash — this gives them their characteristic golden shine. Use a fork to prick each pie once on top, which allows steam to escape and prevents the pastry from puffing unevenly. Bake at 180°C for 25–30 minutes until deep golden brown all over.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Baking Reference

| Stage | Temperature | Time |
|-------|-------------|------|
| Preheat oven | 180°C (356°F) | 10 min before baking |
| Bake pies | 180°C (356°F) | 25–30 minutes |
| Internal temp | 75°C (165°F) | Food safety minimum |

<Tip>
  Meat pies taste best served warm. Reheat at 160°C for 8 minutes if needed.
</Tip>
