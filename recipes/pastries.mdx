  <Tab title="Sausage Roll">
    **Yield:** 30 rolls &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Prep:** 30 min &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Bake:** 20–25 min

    ### Ingredients

    - [ ] 500 g puff pastry (ready-made or house-made)
    - [ ] 400 g sausage meat or seasoned minced pork/beef
    - [ ] 1 tsp mixed herbs
    - [ ] 1 egg (beaten, for egg wash)

    ### Method

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Prepare the Filling & Pastry">
        Roll out your puff pastry on a lightly floured surface into a long rectangle approximately 3–4 mm thick. Spread the sausage meat (or seasoned minced meat) in a firm log along one long edge of the pastry, leaving a small border. Sprinkle the mixed herbs evenly over the meat.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Roll, Seal & Cut">
        Brush the opposite long edge of the pastry with a little egg wash. Roll the pastry tightly over the filling toward the egg-washed edge, pressing firmly to seal the seam underneath. Using a sharp knife, cut the log into individual rolls 8–10 cm in length. Score the tops of each roll with two or three shallow diagonal cuts.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Egg Wash & Bake">
        Place the rolls seam-side down on a lined baking tray. Brush the tops and sides generously with egg wash. Bake at 200°C for 20–25 minutes until the pastry is puffed, golden, and the filling is fully cooked through.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>
      Ensure the sausage meat is seasoned well before rolling — it cannot be adjusted after baking.
    </Note>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Donut">
    **Yield:** 24 donuts &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Prep:** 40 min &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Fry:** 3–4 min per batch

    ### Ingredients

    - [ ] 500 g plain flour
    - [ ] 7 g instant yeast
    - [ ] 80 g sugar
    - [ ] 1 tsp salt
    - [ ] 2 eggs
    - [ ] 200 ml warm milk
    - [ ] 60 g softened butter
    - [ ] Vegetable oil (for frying)
    - [ ] Icing sugar or glazing syrup (for finishing)

    ### Method

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Make the Dough">
        Combine the flour, instant yeast, sugar, and salt in a large mixing bowl and stir to distribute evenly. Create a well in the centre and add the eggs, warm milk, and softened butter. Bring the mixture together into a dough, then knead firmly for approximately 8 minutes — by hand or using a stand mixer with a dough hook — until the dough is smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.
      </Step>

      <Step title="First Prove & Shape">
        Cover the dough with a clean damp cloth or cling film and leave to prove in a warm place for 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size. Once proved, punch the dough down to release the gas. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface, roll it out to approximately 1.5 cm thickness, and cut out rounds using a donut cutter (or two round cutters of different sizes).
      </Step>

      <Step title="Second Prove">
        Arrange the cut donuts on a lightly floured tray, spaced apart to allow for expansion. Cover loosely and prove for a further 30 minutes until visibly puffed and airy.
      </Step>

      <Step title="Fry & Finish">
        Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or fryer to 175°C. Carefully lower the donuts into the oil in small batches and fry for approximately 90 seconds per side until golden brown on both sides. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Once cool enough to handle, dust generously with icing sugar or dip into glazing syrup.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Warning>
      Oil temperature is critical. Below 170°C donuts absorb oil and become greasy. Above 185°C they brown too fast and may be raw inside.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Croissant">
    **Yield:** 16 croissants &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Prep:** 3 hours (incl. lamination) &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Bake:** 18–22 min

    ### Ingredients

    - [ ] 500 g strong white flour
    - [ ] 10 g instant yeast
    - [ ] 10 g salt
    - [ ] 60 g sugar
    - [ ] 300 ml cold milk
    - [ ] 250 g cold unsalted butter (for lamination)
    - [ ] 1 egg (beaten, for egg wash)

    ### Method

    Croissant lamination is a multi-stage process that requires patience and cold temperatures throughout. Follow each step carefully and do not rush the chilling periods.

    - Make a simple dough by combining the flour, yeast, salt, and sugar, then adding the cold milk. Knead briefly — just until the dough is smooth — then flatten it into a square, wrap tightly in cling film, and chill in the refrigerator for 1 hour.
    - Pound the cold butter between two sheets of baking parchment into a flat, even square approximately the same width as the dough. Unwrap the chilled dough and place the butter square in the centre. Fold the dough edges over the butter to encase it completely. You will now perform 3 book folds: roll the dough into a long rectangle, fold it into thirds like a letter, rotate 90°, and repeat. Wrap and chill for 30 minutes between each fold to keep the butter cold and prevent it from breaking through.
    - After the final fold and chill, roll the dough out to approximately 5 mm thickness. Cut it into tall triangles. Starting at the wide base of each triangle, roll up tightly toward the tip, stretching the tip gently as you roll to keep tension. Curve the ends slightly to form the classic crescent shape.
    - Arrange on lined baking trays and prove at room temperature for 1.5–2 hours, until the croissants are visibly puffy and the layers are defined when viewed from the side.
    - Brush each croissant gently with egg wash, taking care not to flatten the layers. Bake at 190°C for 18–22 minutes until deeply golden brown with a crisp, flaky exterior.

    <Tip>
      Good croissants take time. Never rush the lamination process or skip the chilling steps.
    </Tip>
