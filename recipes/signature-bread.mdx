The 5G Silver Bakery signature bread is a soft, golden loaf baked fresh every morning without exception. It is the centrepiece of everything the bakery stands for, and customers return for it daily — which means you must produce it to the exact same standard every single time. Consistency is not optional; it is the product.

## Recipe Metadata

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Yield | 4 standard loaves |
| Prep Time | 30 minutes |
| Proofing Time | 1 hour |
| Bake Time | 35 minutes |
| Total Time | ~2 hours |
| Difficulty | Beginner–Intermediate |

## Ingredients

Before you begin, gather and measure all ingredients. Do not estimate — use scales and measuring jugs for accuracy.

**Dough:**

- [ ] 1 kg strong white bread flour
- [ ] 7 g instant dry yeast (2 sachets)
- [ ] 10 g fine salt
- [ ] 20 g white sugar
- [ ] 60 ml vegetable oil
- [ ] 600 ml warm water (not hot — approx. 38°C)

**For finishing:**

- [ ] 1 egg (beaten, for egg wash)
- [ ] 1 tbsp milk (mix with egg wash for shine)

## Equipment Needed

Make sure the following equipment is clean, dry, and ready before you start:

- Large mixing bowl or stand mixer with dough hook
- Measuring scales and jugs
- 4 standard loaf tins (greased)
- Pastry brush
- Oven thermometer
- Cooling rack

## Step-by-Step Method

<Steps>
  <Step title="Activate the Yeast">
    Combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast in a small bowl or jug. Stir briefly to dissolve, then leave to rest for 10 minutes until the surface becomes frothy and active.

    <Note>
      If the yeast does not froth after 10 minutes, it may be dead — discard the mixture and start again with a fresh sachet of yeast.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Mix the Dough">
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the centre, then pour in the activated yeast mixture and the vegetable oil. Mix everything together until a rough, shaggy dough forms and no dry flour remains at the bottom of the bowl.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Knead the Dough">
    Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead by hand for 10 minutes, or use a stand mixer on medium speed for 7 minutes. The dough is ready when it feels smooth, elastic, and springs back immediately when you poke it with a finger.
  </Step>

  <Step title="First Proof">
    Transfer the dough to a lightly oiled bowl and turn it once to coat the surface. Cover tightly with cling film and place in a warm, draught-free spot for 1 hour, or until the dough has doubled in size.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Shape the Loaves">
    Once risen, punch the dough down firmly to release the gas. Turn it out and divide it into 4 equal portions using a dough scraper or knife. Shape each portion into a smooth log and place it seam-side down into a greased loaf tin.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Second Proof">
    Cover the tins loosely with cling film or a clean damp cloth and allow the dough to rise for a further 30 minutes. While the dough is proofing, preheat your oven to the appropriate temperature (see the Baking Times & Temperatures table below).

    <Warning>
      Do not skip the second proof — this is what gives the bread its light, airy texture. Baking without it will result in a dense, heavy loaf.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Apply Egg Wash">
    In a small bowl, whisk together the beaten egg and milk. Using a pastry brush, gently coat the top surface of each risen loaf with the egg wash. This gives the finished bread its characteristic golden, glossy crust.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Bake">
    Place the loaf tins in the preheated oven. Bake until the tops are deep golden brown and the loaves sound hollow when you remove them from the tin and tap firmly on the bottom. Refer to the temperature table below for exact timings based on your oven type.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Cool">
    Remove the loaves from their tins immediately and transfer to a wire cooling rack. Allow them to cool for at least 20 minutes before slicing or packaging for sale.

    <Warning>
      Never slice hot bread — cutting into a loaf straight from the oven compresses the crumb, ruins the texture, and makes the bread appear dense and underbaked.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Baking Times & Temperatures

Use the table below to match your bake settings to the oven available in your station. If you are unsure which oven type you are using, ask your supervisor before preheating.

| Oven Type | Temperature | Bake Time | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Conventional (top & bottom heat) | 200°C (392°F) | 30–35 min | Place on middle rack |
| Fan/Convection oven | 180°C (356°F) | 28–32 min | Reduce temp by 20°C |
| Deck oven (commercial) | 210°C (410°F) | 25–30 min | Steam injection first 5 min if available |

## Storage & Shelf Life

Once your loaves are fully cooled, handle them according to the following storage rules. Proper storage protects the product and ensures customers always receive fresh bread.

- Store at room temperature in a bread bag or wrapped in cling film.
- Best consumed within 24 hours of baking.
- Do not refrigerate — this accelerates staling and ruins the crumb texture.

<Tip>
  Label every batch with the bake date using masking tape and a marker. This makes it easy for any staff member to identify fresh stock at a glance.
</Tip>
